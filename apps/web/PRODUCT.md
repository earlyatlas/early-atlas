# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Parents, caregivers, home educators, and early-childhood teachers of children
roughly birth to age 6. Mixed expertise — no degree, no account, no paywall.
Usually looking something up **in the moment, often on a phone**: "my three-year-old
— what should they be working on, and what can we actually do right now?" Tired,
busy, and trusting us to be both credible and quick.

## Product Purpose

A free, open, standards-grounded early-childhood curriculum library. People browse
and search by age, stage, domain, and topic to find **skills, activities, printable
worksheets, developmental milestones (Head Start ELOF), and the methodologies** that
inspire lessons. Success = a caregiver finds what a child is working on and a real
thing to do, in well under a minute, and trusts that it's grounded in real research.

## Brand Personality

Warm, calm, trustworthy. The feeling of a well-made children's library or a
thoughtful museum label: grounded and credible, but gentle and approachable. Quiet
confidence, not loud marketing. Voice: plain, encouraging, kind — never clinical,
never cutesy.

## Anti-references

- **Cartoonish daycare** — primary-color clip-art, bubbly rounded display fonts,
  rainbows, balloons, a mascot. We serve the _adults_ supporting children, not the
  children. Warmth must never tip into childishness.
- **Generic AI / SaaS template** — gradient-accent heroes, identical icon+heading+text
  card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, 01/02/03 numbered
  markers, decorative glassmorphism. If it could be any startup, it's wrong.
- Also off-brand: cold corporate sterility, and dense cluttered ed-tech / LMS UIs.

## Design Principles

- **Calm over busy.** Generous whitespace, one clear thing per view, no decoration
  for its own sake. The content — a skill, a milestone, an activity — is the hero.
- **Trust through restraint and evidence.** Credibility comes from clean typography,
  cited sources, and honesty, not from looking flashy.
- **Phone-first, in the moment.** A tired parent on a small screen must scan and tap
  with ease; hierarchy and touch targets come first.
- **Grown-up warmth.** Warmth lives in color temperature, soft surfaces, and kind
  copy — never in childish motifs.
- **Get out of the way.** It is a tool/library; reading and navigating are the job.
  Polish means clarity, hierarchy, legibility, and gentle, purposeful motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Dark and light modes (already token-driven). Body text ≥ 4.5:1
contrast; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; touch targets ≥ 44px. Plain, inclusive
language that welcomes all family structures.
